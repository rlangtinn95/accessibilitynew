Did You Know This About Gjøvik?

Gjøvik is a municipality located in Innlandet county, Norway. It is in the traditional district of Toten. The administrative center of the municipality is the town of Gjøvik. Some of the villages in Gjøvik include Biri, Bybrua and Hunndalen. The 671-square-kilometer municipality is the 169th largest by area out of the 356 municipalities in Norway. Gjøvik has a population of 30,267, which is 35th place by inhabitants in Norway.

Along with Hamar, Lillehammer, Brumunddal and Moelv, Gjøvik is one of the many towns bordering Norway's biggest lake, Mjøsa. The town administration of Gjøvik also includes the suburb area Hunndalen and the rural districts of Biri, Snertingdal and Vardal.

Gjøvik (or Innlandet) has the only paddle steamer boat operating in Norway, which is called PS Skibladner, it sails in lake Mjøsa. Skibladner is a sidewheel design, and her maiden voyage was conducted on 2nd of August 1856, making her the words oldest paddle steamer in timetabled service.

Gjøvik owes much of it's early growth to the local glassworks, which were established by a man named Caspar Kauffeldt in 1807. In the early 19th century, there was considerable immigration there from Valdres and western Norway, aiding Gjøvik's growth.
